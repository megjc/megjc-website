Quality of Life linked to the quality of the Natural Environment – H.M. Vaz

Minister without Portfolio in the Ministry of Economic Growth and Job Creation, Hon. Daryl Vaz, has stated that while the Government is pursuing its economic (‘5 in 4’) growth targets, it is mindful that the quality of life for all Jamaicans is linked to the quality of the natural environment.

In his remarks delivered by, Mr. Locksley Smith, Senior Advisor in the Ministry of Economic Growth and Job Creation, at the 24th Anniversary celebrations of the Blue and John Crow Mountains as a National Park, Minister Vaz gave the assurance that the government cannot allow adverse environmental impacts and climate change to disrupt the lives of Jamaicans.

As such, we intend to increase our efforts to pursue long term transformative development and accelerate sustainable climate –resilient economic growth” the Minister said.

The Minister noted that in addition to the Blue and John Crow Mountains National Park, Jamaica has three Marine Parks and more than 200 protected areas, which include natural forest reserves and fish sanctuaries, all of which must be managed sustainably in order to enhance the quality of life for Jamaicans..

‘My Ministry and our agency, the National Environment and Planning Agency (NEPA), are currently finalizing a Protected Areas Policy and overarching legislation for the management of the protected areas system network”, the Minister said.

He added that the draft National Forest Management and Conservation Plan (2016-2026) was recently completed and is currently being discussed in islandwide public consultations, adding that twelve (12) additional forest reserves are to be declared shortly.

In pledging the Government’s co-operation with all environmental stakeholders, Minister Vaz thanked Dr. Susan Otuokan and her team at the Jamaica Conservation Development Trust (JCDT) for their efforts in preserving and conserving the Blue and John Crow Mountains National Park and in raising awareness about the value of the Park to all Jamaicans.

The Blue and John Crow Mountains National Park was designated a World Heritage Site in 2015 by the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO). The declaration highlights the area as an outstanding example of biodiversity and ecological processes, as well as cultural resources.
